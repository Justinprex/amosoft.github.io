About

I am a reverse engineer specializing in embedded systems, firmware analysis, and low-level debugging. This site is a collection of my case studies, write-ups, and experiments across hardware and software reverse engineering. My focus is on practical demonstrations of techniques, and breaking down complex topics into clear, applicable steps.

The goal of this portfolio is to showcase what can be achieved through methodical analysis of firmware, hardware protocols, and security implementations. I also aim to document lessons learned, methodologies, and useful tooling for other engineers and researchers.

Areas of Expertise

  • Firmware reverse engineering (ARM Cortex, MIPS, x86)
  • Debugging and protocol analysis (SWD, JTAG, UART, SPI, I²C)
  • Binary analysis, disassembly, and decompilation (Ghidra, IDA Pro, Radare2)
  • Exploit research and vulnerability discovery in embedded devices

Professional Engagements

I provide consulting and training for organizations and individuals who want to strengthen their capabilities in reverse engineering and firmware security. This includes hands-on instruction, project-based collaboration, and technical reviews of embedded systems.

Selected Talks & Contributions

  • 2025: Workshop on firmware unpacking and cryptographic protocol analysis (private training)
  • 2024: Internal seminar on JTAG/UART exploitation in consumer devices
  • 2023: Guest contributor to open-source tooling for memory analysis and dump automation
